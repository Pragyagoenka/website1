  • Samsung PRISM Project
Goal
2021
User-Experience Design                                Team Project
The aim of this project was to bridge the gap between multiple video editors to provide a unified, seamless editing experience to Samsung users.
This was a collaborative UX design project between Samsung Research Institute-Bangalore and my college NID AP. For the project we were divided into interdisciplinary teams of 5. The focus of the project was on creative of ultra-short narratives.

Being one of the only two with some prior experience in UX, I was responsible for planning the project i.e. what will be our next step keeping in mind the given feedback, research, synthesis, ideation and prototyping.
Design Brief
Designing an engaging User Experience for Samsung editing interface which is intuitive, straight forward and less time consuming for the amateur editors.


Problem Statements
  • The various features are scattered and are difficult to discover for the users which create break in user's editing journey.
  • Little to no room for error prevention and recovery, the user has to start all over again.
  • Despite of having a large array of editing features, users are not able to utilize them properly, due to low recognition of features.


Our approach
Our final concept focused on unifying the user's editor journey, by introducing micro-interactions and gamifying the experience of creation and editing media.

Something I learned


  • Feedback oriented & user centric design process

    We found ourselves constantly going back and forth from testing and feedback to design, and it was fun to be involved in such a feedback oriented & user centric design process, compared to the smooth design process we are accustomed to in academics.
  • Multi-disciplinary collaboration

    Being one of the two students who had gone through a UX project in the past, I had to find a balance among planning our next steps, teaching the ones who didnâ€™t have the experience and designing for the project. That led to a better understanding of the concepts. Meanwhile, learning how my friends from other disciplines view and approach the same problem.
  • Convenience & choice

    Through our discussions we all would often land up arguing about the balance between convenience & choice, which I realize at the heart of many UX problems as well as solutions.     It was fun getting first-hand experience of uncovering the needs of a proper client (Samsung), communicating ideas clearly, talking about constraints and requirements, and meeting expectations. This was very different from the more boundless academic projects.

Goal
2022
Technically Complex Product Design                   Academic Project
To design an electric kettle for Urban Upper middle-class North Indian household to be used in winters, keeping child safety in mind, enhances the user interaction and fit modern home aesthetics.
This was an individual academic project. We had the constraint to re-design a consumer electronic without introducing any new technology, and keeping the basic structure of the product intact. As part of the project, the design should be detailed enough to be manufactured by commonly available technology in the industry.

I chose an electric kettle as, I felt it would be good challenge to re-design a product which was perfected over centuries, and being re-imaged till date. Also, the fact to design a tea based product for a chai drinking community, was a prime scenario to explore transformation od a product with context.

Goal
2022
Interaction design and Development
To design and create an online portfolio to showcase my professional work on the internet.
Yes, I not only designed this website but also coded it from scratch in Visual studio code. And you might wonder "Pragya, you are a designer, why waste time learning a skill, you will likely never use?"

I didnâ€™t brave rivers to get to school or discovered America but I surely like to do things to satisfy my curiosity. My keen interest in technology and the desire to achieve the exact design I had in mind, (without it being bended to the templates available with existing web building tools), basically forced me to develop this website from scratch.

The Process

Compilation

I started by looking back at all my projects and choosing my favorite 8.
Then for each individual project, I have to decide what goes up in te page and how, as placing all research would make it too long.

Next I got on the internet and looked at different portfolio websites for inspirations.

Ideation

After research, I was brimming with the exciting possible directions I could have take. I used figma to materialize them as much as I could.

Development

  • I learned basic HTML, CSS & Js.
  • I googled how to achieve particular effects.
  • Understand how the code worked
  • Tweaked and customised it to my liking.
  • Error rectification

Compilation


It was a true challenge to dissociate myself from my own work and see them objectively (keeping the self-loathe away). Next task was to re-image them for an audience.

Research
I looked at a wide variety of aesthetics and digital trends. As I wanted to keep this website for sometime, I decided to not hop on a fad blindly.
After exploring a reasonable breadth of aesthetics, it was time for some introspection to decide what I personally connect to and is best to represent my work.

I decided to go with brutalism.

Brutalism unapologetically strips things down to their very essence and is known for its industrial, raw look and use of simple geometric shapes. However, brutalism offers more than just pure functionality. It also provides us with highly enjoyable aesthetic experiences.

I felt it would a timeless approach, focusing on my work rather than the website carrying it.


Ideation

Final concept



Why vertical navigation tho?
First, I'm not saying that one is better than the other. A navigation that works well in one context, may not work as well in another. I chose a vertical navigation as it worked with my needs better.

Adding a horizontal division to a landscape screen, leaves a content area with 1. Very distorted ratio and 2. Less height forcing the user to scroll more. Other than this personal preference, one simple google search will tell you the perks of vertical nav bar and the cons weren't really a problem for this kind of website. I wanted to tell a story, a vertical navbar not only gives the user a sneak peek into the chapters but also indicate where are you in the narrative.

Why this weird interactive background?
I wanted to add something interesting to my website, and what could be better than a small interaction element based on my deep appreciation for tessellations and general theory of relativity. The aesthetics where very simple call to remind the audience of space. As the cursor moves through the canvas it distorts the tessellation. Parallel to how the presence of matter and energy curved the very fabric of this spacetime.

Trying to remain true to my brutalist aesthetics I kept it as simple and minimal as I could.
As I'm no expert in this developing arena, if you found any bug or feedback, please me through the links given below. Also, you are interacting with the product now, I'll save some digital real-estate here and ask you to explore the site to see the outcome.

Something I learned


  • HTML, CSS & Js

    I just didn't learn these languages as 3 languages, but as an intersection while trying to make a product. I learned about theories, creative problem solving and so many new concepts in general. And I'm not just saying this to justify the enormously extra time, I 'invested' in the exercise.

  • Data Structures & Responsive design

    (I hope this becomes relevant in future)I gained a new insight into data structure and how actually responsive design works. It much more that the changed aspect ratio, it changes how people perceive the same element, and we are suppose to design each component for these multiple expectations without losing the whole.

  • When to ask for help

    Yeah I learned a few things, but in the end I'm a designer. This is in no way a limitation, but a good designer is nothing without collaboration. I had to ask multiple times for help and feedback from my engineering friends. We brainstormed together on problems, & it was fun to see the problem with a different lens for a while.

  • Listen to yourself

    It is not necessarily a learning but, it was fun to make something for yourself. I extremely enjoyed going crazy on everything. Coming up with answers to every new problem or question. All out I felt extremely happy. I'm not this self-narcissistic, but this was a nice journey of self realization.

Goal
2020
Upcycle Project
To make a useful seating out of the broken and discarded chair, in the given time of 1 week.
This classic piece of office furniture was about to be sold to our lovely 'kabadiwale bhaiya', for it's heavy metal structure. But how can I, the resident annoying 'sustainable' designer let this happen?! And that is how my parents challenged me to make something useful out of this chair, ina week's time.
So, here you may wonder 'It was already going to be in one kind of recycling unit, what makes your efforts anymore sustainable Pragya?'
My answer is simple sustainability goes beyond conservation of materials. Our perception of sustainability should extend to energy as well. How much energy is consumed to manufacture & later transport the product. Hence, my aim was simple be as creative & lazy as possible.
The chair



Condition
  • The wooden frames were broken, so a simple re-meshing won't work.
  • The metal frame is heavy and robust, but slippery for weaving directly on it.


The Process


De-construction
I removed all the frames, and accessories. Once I had the bare frame I started ideating what could be done with minimal changes.


Re-construction
I used the existing holes for screws to make the weaving yarn stay for seating. Also, the back rods are cut to form bottom support, which acts as foot rest and additional storage.

Final product



I used cotton locally available cords for seating, this not only is sustainable but budget friendly too. The specific choice of a woven seating was to inspired by local 'khat', giving local craft a new take.

Something I learned


  • Quick design project

    I had less than a week to not only design but to make the seating as well. This required me to think quick on my feet, improvise on the spot, and manage the resources efficiently.
  • Limited manipulation

    Since it was peak of 2nd lock-down, I had to find creative work around for the absence of proper tools and machinery. I had one trip to get the frame to my liking and buy all the raw materials.
